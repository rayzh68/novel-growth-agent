# Cultural Concepts

## Family & Social Structure
-   **Patriarchal Authority:** The Su family operates under a strict patriarchal system where the grandfather (Su Laoer/Old Master Su) holds ultimate authority, followed by the eldest son (Arthur Blackwood). Women, especially wives and unmarried daughters, have limited agency.
- **Filial Piety ():** A core value, often exploited. Children are expected to obey and care for their parents and elders. Elara Willow's defiance and eventual estrangement from her biological father are major transgressions in this context. Su Jinquan beating his father is a severe act of filial impiety.
- **Main Wife vs. Concubine ():** Eleanor Maeve is the main wife, while Zhou-shi is a 'flat wife' (), a status between a main wife and a concubine, but still subordinate to the main wife. Concubinage () is a common practice for wealthy men, and a woman's insistence on monogamy () is a modern concept that clashes with traditional norms.
- **Disowning/Severing Ties ():** A severe social punishment where individuals are cast out of the family clan, losing all familial support and protection. Elara Willow and Eleanor Maeve actively seek to sever ties with the Su clan.
- **Reputation ():** Crucial for individuals and families, especially for women's marriage prospects. Accusations of inauspiciousness, adultery, or unfilial conduct can ruin a person's life.
- **Gender Roles:** Women are primarily expected to manage the household, bear children (especially sons), and maintain family harmony. Business ventures by women are unusual and often looked down upon. Daughters are sometimes seen as 'money-losing goods' ().
- **Social Hierarchy ():** A rigid class system where scholars () are at the top, followed by farmers (), artisans (), and merchants (). This impacts marriage prospects and social standing.
- **Master-Servant Relationships:** Servants can be bound by 'lifetime contracts' (), highlighting a lack of personal freedom. Elara Willow's approach to her staff, offering good pay and bonuses, reflects a more modern management style.

## Superstition & Beliefs
- **Inauspiciousness:** Elara Willow's six fingers () and birth on the eve of the Ghost Festival (making her an 'yin person' ) are considered highly inauspicious, leading to her being labeled a 'broom star' () and 'monster' ().
- **Ghosts & Hauntings:** The 'ghost house' () is believed to be haunted by the spirit of a wronged woman (), influencing villagers' perceptions and fears. Elara Willow strategically uses these rumors.
- **Shamans & Geomancy ():** Spirit mediums and geomancers are consulted for misfortune, exorcisms, and fortune-telling. Their predictions can heavily influence family decisions.
- **Lost Teeth & Afterlife:** A belief that losing teeth in a certain way can affect one's reincarnation ().
- **Fated with Ill Omens ():** Kaelen Sterling is believed to be fated with ill omens, contributing to his solitary nature.

## Marriage & Relationships
- **Matchmakers ():** Essential for arranging marriages, which are often based on family status, wealth, and reputation rather than love.
-   **Dowry:** A significant part of marriage negotiations, reflecting the bride's family wealth and status.
-   **Forced Marriage:** Families can force individuals into undesirable marriages for financial gain or to resolve disputes. Elara Willow faces a plot for a forced marriage to a known abuser.
- **Mutual Divorce ():** A rare but possible form of divorce initiated by mutual agreement, offering more dignity than a husband's unilateral 'letter of divorce' ().
- **Love Rivals:** The 'white lotus' () archetype is used for outwardly pure but manipulative love rivals.

## Justice & Governance
- **Yamen ():** The local government office, responsible for law enforcement and legal proceedings. Magistrates (, ) and constables () hold authority.
-   **Legal Manipulation:** Elara Willow demonstrates a willingness to use legal loopholes, fabricate evidence, and leverage influence to achieve justice or revenge.
- **Punishments:** Severe punishments exist, such as 'pig cage drowning' () for premarital pregnancy and 'dismemberment by five horses' () for severe crimes.

## Business & Economy
- **Traditional Medicine Halls ():** Important establishments for health and commerce.
-   **Agriculture:** Farming is a primary livelihood. Introduction of new crops like chili and tomato can significantly impact local economy.
- **Gambling Dens ():** Destructive forces that can ruin families, leading to debt and exploitation.
- **'Possessing a Treasure Makes One Guilty' ():** A concept where having valuable assets (like Elara Willow's Ejiao formula) makes one a target for exploitation if they lack powerful protection.
