# Chapter Range: 001-050

## Range Summary
- **Range function:** This range establishes the protagonist, Elara Willow, as a modern transmigrator in an ancient, abusive family. It details her initial adaptation, her fierce defiance against her oppressors, and her strategic efforts to protect her mother and sister. The primary goal is to set the stage for their eventual escape and pursuit of independence.
- **Main emotional/commercial purpose:** To engage the reader with Elara Willow's struggle against overwhelming injustice, her cleverness in navigating a hostile environment, and her unwavering determination to secure a better life for her family. It builds strong emotional investment in the plight of Madam Chen and Piper Rose, and anticipation for Elara Willow's future triumphs.
- **Key anti-drift constraints:**
    - Elara Willow's modern mindset should drive her actions and internal thoughts but must be grounded in the ancient world's realities and limitations.
    - The pervasive patriarchal oppression, superstition, and medical backwardness of the setting must be consistently portrayed as significant obstacles.
    - The antagonists' malice and the severity of the abuse must be clear, serving as motivation for Elara Willow's actions, without becoming gratuitous.
    - Madam Chen's traditional beliefs and her gradual, painful shift towards embracing independence must be believable and earned.
    - No significant romantic developments or large-scale business ventures should occur within this range.
