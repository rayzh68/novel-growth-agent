# Adaptation Locks: Names

This document establishes the production English name lock pack for Rewrite, Review, and Finalize agents for Project ID: project_001. It ensures consistent, commercially viable Western-style English names for characters, families, locations, and titles, preventing name drift and preserving core narrative functions.

## 1. Naming Strategy

**Full Western Adaptation Policy:** All character names, family names, and significant entity names will be fully adapted to Western-style English. This includes using English first names and surnames.

**Why Pinyin and Chinese Surnames are Not Used in Production Prose:** To align with US/UK romance and female web fiction market expectations, and to enhance commercial readability and accessibility, traditional Chinese naming conventions (including pinyin and Chinese surnames) are strictly avoided in the final production prose. This ensures names feel natural and familiar to the target audience.

**How Family Separation is Preserved After Westernization:** Family relationships and household structures are preserved through distinct adapted surnames and clear relationship locks.
*   The heroine's toxic birth family (formerly "Su family") will share a distinct adapted surname.
*   The heroine's new chosen household will have a unique identifier, often using the heroine's adapted surname.
*   The male lead's noble family will have a distinct adapted noble surname.
*   Merchant/business ally families will have their own unique adapted surnames.
*   Maternal relatives will share a distinct adapted surname.
*   Antagonistic noble families will have their own distinct adapted surnames.
Changes in household status (e.g., marriage, divorce, adoption) are explicitly stated in the character's "Family / Household Lock" and "Relationship Lock."

**How Noble Titles, Military Titles, Village Roles, Shops, and Businesses are Handled:**
*   **Noble Titles:** Adapted to their closest Western equivalents (e.g., Marquis, Marchioness, Lord, Lady, Heir Apparent).
*   **Military Titles:** Adapted to Western military ranks (e.g., General, Captain).
*   **Village Roles:** Adapted to clear English terms (e.g., Village Elder, Scholar, Doctor, Matchmaker).
*   **Shops and Businesses:** Given evocative English names that reflect their function or nature.

**How to Prevent Name Drift:**
*   All adapted names are locked in this document and must be used consistently across all production stages.
*   A comprehensive list of forbidden pinyin and Chinese source names is provided.
*   A QA checklist ensures adherence to naming policies.
*   Any new character or entity introduced must be assigned an adapted name following these rules and added to this lock file.
