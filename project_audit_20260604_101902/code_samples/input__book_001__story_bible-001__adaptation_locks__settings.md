# Settings Adaptation Locks

## Locations

### Da'ao Village ()
*   **Setting Function:** Elara Willow's initial home and the primary setting for her early struggles and rise. A traditional, rural, and superstitious community that provides both obstacles and opportunities. It is the source of local gossip and social pressure, and later the base for Elara Willow's successful businesses.
*   **Recurring Scenes:** Family arguments at the Su Old Residence, gossip among villagers, Elara Willow's daily life and business operations at her new home.
*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** Da'ao Village
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **Rural Authenticity:** Must maintain its traditional, rural character. Drifting towards a more modernized or less superstitious village would undermine the initial challenges and cultural context.
    *   **Gossip Hub:** Its role as a center for village gossip and social pressure is crucial for character development and plot progression.
*   **Confidence Level:** High

### Su Old Residence ()
*   **Setting Function:** The ancestral home of the Su family, representing patriarchal control, traditional family structure, and the source of oppression against Chen Shi and Elara Willow. Later becomes a symbol of the main Su family's decline.
*   **Recurring Scenes:** Frequent family arguments, confrontations, meals, and attempts by Huang Shi and Zhou Shi to assert dominance or exploit Elara Willow.
*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** Su Family Manor / Old Su House
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **Symbol of Oppression:** Its function as the primary site of Elara Willow's and Chen Shi's oppression must be preserved. Softening its negative portrayal would weaken the protagonist's motivation for independence.
    *   **Decline:** The eventual decline and internal strife within the Old Residence are critical for showing the consequences of the antagonists' actions.
*   **Confidence Level:** High

### Beiaozhi Ghost House ()
*   **Setting Function:** Elara Willow's first independent home after her divorce. Initially feared due to rumors of a haunting spirit (Lu Shi), which allows Elara Willow to acquire it cheaply. It becomes a symbol of her defiance, resourcefulness, and a safe haven for her family, later expanding into a comfortable home and base for her businesses (e.g., chili cultivation).
*   **Recurring Scenes:** Family discussions and planning for business, cooking scenes, and occasional encounters with villagers curious about the "ghost."
*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** North Hollow Ghost House / Su Family's New Home
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **Ghost Rumors:** The initial reputation as a "ghost house" is a key plot device for its acquisition and for deterring unwanted visitors. Removing or significantly altering this aspect would impact the narrative.
    *   **Symbol of Independence:** Its role as Elara Willow's first independent home is central to her character arc.
*   **Confidence Level:** High

### Ao Mountain ()
*   **Setting Function:** A dangerous but resource-rich wilderness area adjacent to Da'ao Village. It is the home of Kaelen Sterling and a source of wild game and medicinal herbs. It is the site of Elara Willow's first significant encounter with Kaelen Sterling.
*   **Recurring Scenes:** Kaelen Sterling hunting, Elara Willow foraging for herbs or ingredients, encounters between Elara Willow and Kaelen Sterling.
*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** Ao Mountain / Hollow Mountain
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **Wilderness & Danger:** Must maintain its character as a wild and potentially dangerous place, essential for Kaelen Sterling's hunter persona and for plot points involving wild animals.
    *   **Resource Source:** Its role as a source of natural resources for Elara Willow's businesses (e.g., herbs, game) is important.
*   **Confidence Level:** High

### Baise Town ()
*   **Setting Function:** The nearest town, serving as a hub for trade, commerce, and local administration. It is where Arthur Blackwood works and where Elara Willow establishes her initial food stalls and later her restaurant, expanding her business ventures. It also contains the yamen (county office) and the influential Zhou Mansion.
*   **Recurring Scenes:** Market scenes, Elara Willow selling her products, encounters with various townspeople, business meetings.
*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** White Stone Town / Baise Town
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **Commercial Hub:** Its function as a commercial center is vital for Elara Willow's business growth.
    *   **Social Hierarchy:** The distinction between village and town life, and the presence of local officials and powerful families, is crucial for illustrating social dynamics.
*   **Confidence Level:** High

### Qing Ji Seed Shop ()
*   **Setting Function:** A key business owned by legacy source character, serving as a source of seeds, agricultural knowledge, and a crucial business partner for Elara Willow's innovative farming ventures (e.g., chili and tomato cultivation).
*   **Recurring Scenes:** Business meetings between Elara Willow and legacy source character, discussions about new crops and agricultural techniques.
*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** Green Mark Seed Shop / Qingji Grain Store
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **Business Partnership:** Its role as a consistent and supportive business partner for Elara Willow is critical.
    *   **Agricultural Innovation:** The shop's connection to new crops and farming methods is central to Elara Willow's economic rise.
*   **Confidence Level:** High

### Baicaotang ()
*   **Setting Function:** A prominent pharmacy/herbal medicine shop, implicitly owned by legacy source character's family. It becomes a significant distribution channel for Elara Willow's Ejiao, linking her traditional medicine knowledge to a wider market.
*   **Recurring Scenes:** Elara Willow delivering Ejiao, discussions with the manager (Manager Fu) about production and sales.
*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** Hundred Herbs Hall / Baicaotang Pharmacy
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **Ejiao Distribution:** Its role as the primary outlet for Elara Willow's Ejiao is essential for her financial success.
    *   **Connection to Song Family:** Its ownership by the influential Song family provides Elara Willow with a powerful backing.
*   **Confidence Level:** High

### Chen Family Village ()
*   **Setting Function:** Chen Shi's maiden home, initially a place of abandonment and later a site of her family's opportunistic attempts to exploit Elara Willow's newfound wealth. It highlights the breakdown of traditional family obligations and the harsh realities of poverty.
*   **Recurring Scenes:** Hostile visits by Elara Willow and Chen Shi, confrontations with Chen Shi's relatives, attempts at extortion.
*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** Chen Village
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **Source of Exploitation:** Its function as a source of antagonistic, exploitative relatives for Chen Shi must be maintained. Softening their greed would undermine Chen Shi's character arc and Elara Willow's protective nature.
*   **Confidence Level:** High

### Changping County ()
*   **Setting Function:** A higher administrative center than Baise Town, where the County Magistrate resides. It represents a larger market and a more formal legal system. Arthur Blackwood works here, and Elara Willow's businesses expand into this area.
*   **Recurring Scenes:** Legal proceedings at the yamen, Elara Willow conducting business, interactions with officials.
*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** Changping County
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **Administrative Center:** Its role as a seat of local government and a larger market is important for demonstrating the expansion of Elara Willow's influence and the legal challenges she faces.
*   **Confidence Level:** High

### Shangjing ()
*   **Setting Function:** The capital city, representing the pinnacle of society, political power, and wealth. It is the home of Kaelen Sterling's noble family (Guangning Marquisate) and the future setting for Elara Willow's expansion into high society and confrontation with powerful adversaries. It also houses Kaelen Sterling's various businesses.
*   **Recurring Scenes:** Noble family gatherings, political intrigues, Elara Willow managing Kaelen Sterling's businesses, social events.
*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** The Capital / Shangjing
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **Political & Social Apex:** Its status as the capital and center of power is crucial for the overarching political intrigue and the challenges Elara Willow faces in high society.
    *   **Noble Family Base:** It is the primary location for Kaelen Sterling's noble family and their machinations.
*   **Confidence Level:** High

### Guangning Marquisate ()
*   **Setting Function:** Kaelen Sterling's birth family's residence in Shangjing. It is a center of noble family politics, power struggles, and a source of significant opposition to Elara Willow due to her commoner background.
*   **Recurring Scenes:** Family meetings, confrontations between Kaelen Sterling's stepmother/stepbrother and Elara Willow, social events.
*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** Marquis Guangning's Estate
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **Noble Intrigue:** Its function as the primary site of noble family conflicts and opposition to Elara Willow is central to the later plot.
    *   **Social Status Barrier:** The Marquisate represents the social barrier Elara Willow must overcome to marry Kaelen Sterling.
*   **Confidence Level:** High

### Cuizhuyuan Tea House ()
*   **Setting Function:** One of Kaelen Sterling's businesses in Shangjing, initially mismanaged but later taken over and revitalized by Elara Willow. It serves as a hub for information, social gatherings, and a base for Elara Willow's operations in the capital.
*   **Recurring Scenes:** Elara Willow managing staff, business meetings, gathering information, social interactions.
*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** Emerald Bamboo Tea House
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **Business Revitalization:** Its transformation under Elara Willow's management is a key demonstration of her business acumen.
    *   **Information Hub:** Its role as a place for gathering intelligence in the capital is important for plot progression.
*   **Confidence Level:** High

### Mobei Border ()
*   **Setting Function:** The distant frontier where the war against the Tartars takes place. It is where Kaelen Sterling serves as a general, representing danger, military conflict, and the period of separation from Elara Willow.
*   **Recurring Scenes:** War reports, Kaelen Sterling's military campaigns, discussions about the war's impact.
*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** Mobei Frontier / Northern Border
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **War Zone:** Its portrayal as a dangerous war zone is crucial for Kaelen Sterling's character arc and the stakes of his separation from Elara Willow.
    *   **Geopolitical Context:** The war's significance for the empire and its impact on the characters' lives must be maintained.
*   **Confidence Level:** High

### Yunzhou ()
*   **Setting Function:** Chen Ye's ancestral home, where he travels to reclaim his identity and pursue his studies for the imperial examinations. It represents his personal journey of self-discovery and ambition.
*   **Recurring Scenes:** Chen Ye's studies, interactions with his birth family (implied).
*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** Yunzhou
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **Chen Ye's Arc:** Its role as the setting for Chen Ye's personal and academic journey is essential.
*   **Confidence Level:** High

### Cao Family Residence ()
*   **Setting Function:** The home of legacy source character's wealthy and influential clan. It becomes Chen Shi's new, supportive family environment after her remarriage, providing stability and prosperity for her and her daughters.
*   **Recurring Scenes:** Family gatherings, discussions about Cao family business and politics, Chen Shi's new life.
*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** Cao Family Estate
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **Supportive Environment:** Its portrayal as a stable and supportive environment for Chen Shi is crucial, contrasting with the Su Old Residence.
    *   **Influence:** The Cao family's wealth and influence are important for their role in the story.
*   **Confidence Level:** High

### Marquis Zhennan Residence ()

*   **Suggested Adapted Place Name:** Marquis Zhennan Manor
*   **Forbidden Location Drift Risks:**
    *   **Symbol of Status:** Its role as the official residence of a Marquis is critical for reflecting Kaelen Sterling's and Elara Willow's elevated social standing.
